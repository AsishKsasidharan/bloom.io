Field Notes
Deploying Argo Floats in the Bay of Bengal
Argo floats provide autonomous temperature and salinity profiles across the global ocean and are critical for monitoring upper-ocean variability in the Bay of Bengal.

How Argo Floats Work

Argo floats descend to depths of around 2000 meters, drift with ocean currents, then rise back to the surface while recording temperature and salinity profiles.



Importance in the Bay of Bengal

Argo observations help monitor:

• Freshwater stratification • Barrier layer formation • Cyclone-induced mixing • Seasonal monsoon variability • Upper ocean heat content



Observational Campaigns

Field deployments in the northern Bay of Bengal improve understanding of salinity-driven processes and ocean-atmosphere interactions.
